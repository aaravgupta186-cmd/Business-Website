Financial Literacy
Budgeting Basics

Budgeting helps track income, expenses, and savings so individuals can avoid overspending. It supports decision-making by showing where money should be allocated first. A basic budget includes fixed costs (rent), variable costs (food), and planned savings. By reviewing spending each month, people stay financially stable and prepared for emergencies.

Managing Money

Effective money management ensures spending does not exceed income and savings grow steadily. It includes tracking spending, setting financial goals, and avoiding unnecessary debt. People manage money better by prioritizing needs over wants and planning for future expenses. Good habits—like saving monthly and reviewing bank statements—support financial security.

Entrepreneurial Mindset

An entrepreneurial mindset focuses on problem-solving, innovation, and taking calculated risks. Entrepreneurs look for gaps in society and create solutions that bring value to people. They also build resilience—learning from failures and improving their ideas. This mindset encourages initiative, adaptability, and long-term planning.
